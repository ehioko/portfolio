Overview
Thrive is a a mental health platform specifically for Black and Hispanic communities, addressing the urgent need for accessible, culturally sensitive support. Through features like personalized mood tracking, check-ins, and cognitive behavioral therapy games, Thrive empowers users to manage their mental health in an engaging, supportive space.
Milo, our AI therapist, offers affordable, consistent guidance at just $5 a month, filling a gap that mainstream mental health apps miss. My design approach focused on ease of use and cultural relevance, with community forums and gamified tools that keep users engaged. Targeting HBCUs and HSIs, Thrive aims to increase access to mental health services and reduce suicide rates by 20% over five years.
CH. 1 // WHAT THIS IS ALL ABOUT
Thrive is a mental health platform uniquely tailored for Black and Hispanic communities, designed to offer accessible, culturally relevant mental health support. With features like mood tracking, personalized activities, and interactive AI therapy, Thrive provides a safe space for users to explore and manage their mental well-being.
CH. 2 // THE IDEA BEHIND IT
Recognizing the mental health disparities and stigma that often prevent Black and Hispanic students from seeking help, Thrive was built to address these unique challenges. By creating a platform that is both affordable and culturally sensitive, Thrive bridges the gap in mental health care for communities in need.
CH. 3 // THE EXECUTION
Through multiple design iterations, I focused on creating an intuitive, inclusive, and empathetic user experience. Thrive’s interface offers a seamless and supportive journey, from the scientifically-based initial survey to real-time interactions with Milo, our AI therapist, ensuring every interaction feels personal and empowering.
Discovery
Defining The Problem
Thrive addresses the challenge of limited, culturally sensitive mental health resources for Black and Hispanic communities. Despite high rates of mental health struggles, these communities often face barriers in finding affordable and relevant support. Thrive aims to close this gap by providing an accessible, stigma-free space that resonates with users' unique cultural backgrounds and experiences.

Competition Overview

HBCU Business Pitch Competition hosted by The Alabama Collective, where over teams developed innovative business ideas aimed at addressing specific community needs. Competing against over 40 teams, we presented a compelling business model, market strategy, and prototype, aiming to positively impact mental health outcomes for students at HBCUs and beyond.

Role

As the Product Designer, I collaborated closely with my team to conceptualize our mental health app’s core features and user experience. I designed a detailed prototype in Figma, focusing on accessibility, ease of use, and cultural relevance to resonate with our target audience. My role involved translating our ideas into a visually engaging and functional design that reflected our mission to support and empower students through technology.
